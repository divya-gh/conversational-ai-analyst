## what is intent taxonomy?
An intent taxonomy is a structured, hierarchical list of all the possible user intents your conversational AI or agent needs to understand. It’s essentially the map of what your system can recognize and respond to.

Think of it as the official catalog of user intentions, organized in a clean, logical way.

⭐ Why it matters
A good intent taxonomy ensures:

Consistent labeling across datasets

Clear separation between similar intents

Better model performance

Easier debugging and analytics

Smooth handoff between teams (data, engineering, product)

For someone building AI agents (like you), it’s the backbone of your NLU system.

🧠 What an intent taxonomy looks like
It’s usually structured like this:

Level 1: Categories
Broad themes

Account Management

Payments

Technical Support

Product Discovery

Level 2: Intents
Specific user goals

Reset Password

Update Email

Check Payment Status

Troubleshoot Login

Find Product Availability

Level 3: Sub‑intents (optional)
More granular variations

Reset Password → Forgot Password

Reset Password → Change Password

🧩 Why teams build intent taxonomies
To avoid overlapping intents

To reduce confusion for annotators

To ensure training data is consistent

To make analytics meaningful (e.g., “Top 10 intents this month”)

To scale conversational systems without chaos
